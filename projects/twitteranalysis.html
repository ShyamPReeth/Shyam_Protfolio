Depression Symptom Identification

  • Tech Stack: Python, Machine Learning, Transfer Learning (TL), Grid Search (GS), Multi-Layer Perceptron (MLP)
  • Dataset: RAVDESS, TASS

This study explores the use of acoustic characteristics of speech for identifying depressive symptoms using Transfer Learning (TL). Features like rhythm, pitch, and tone are analyzed to detect depression-related emotions.

Initially, a Multi-Layer Perceptron (MLP) network trained on the RAVDESS dataset achieved 65% accuracy. Further hyperparameter tuning using Grid Search (GS) improved accuracy to 71% for emotions linked to depression, such as neutrality, sadness, anger, fear, and disgust.

In the final phase, the optimized MLP classifier with TL was applied to the TASS dataset, achieving an impressive accuracy of 99.80%, demonstrating the model's effectiveness in predicting high-risk depression cases.

This research highlights the power of TL in advancing mental health diagnostics, offering a robust approach to speech-based depression prediction with superior accuracy over previous methodologies.
